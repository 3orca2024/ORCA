Major Characters (WHO they are & WHY they matter)

Theseus

Duke of Athens. Represents law, order, and reason. His authority drives the conflict by supporting Egeus’ demand that Hermia marry Demetrius.

Hippolyta

Queen of the Amazons, engaged to Theseus. She is calmer and more empathetic than Theseus and often questions his rigid thinking.

Hermia

Loves Lysander. Strong-willed and defiant. Challenges patriarchal authority by refusing to marry Demetrius.

Lysander

Loves Hermia. Romantic and rebellious. Represents true love before magic interferes.

Demetrius

Originally loved Helena but now pursues Hermia. Represents shallow, changeable love.

Helena

Loves Demetrius. Insecure, emotional, and self-critical. Believes love equals suffering.

Oberon

King of the Fairies. Controls magic and manipulation. Uses enchantment to restore balance, but often causes chaos first.

Titania

Queen of the Fairies. Strong, proud, and protective. Her conflict with Oberon causes disorder in nature.

Puck (Robin Goodfellow)

Mischievous fairy. Loves chaos and mistakes. Represents unpredictability and comedy.

The Mechanicals (Comedy Purpose)

Bottom – Weaver, overconfident, comic relief. Represents foolish ambition.

Quince – Director of the play. Serious but ineffective.

Flute – Plays Thisbe, embarrassed due to his beard.

Snug – Plays Lion, worried about remembering lines.

Starveling – Plays Moonshine.

Major Themes (WHY scenes matter)

  • Love vs. Reason – Love is irrational and chaotic.
  • Dream vs. Reality – The forest blurs what is real.
  • Order vs. Chaos – Athens = law, Forest = disorder.
  • Appearance vs. Truth – Characters misjudge love and loyalty.

Key Quotes (Speaker + Significance)

"Lord, what fools these mortals be!"

Speaker: Puck

Significance: Shows Puck’s amusement at human love and highlights the play’s theme of foolishness.

"The course of true love never did run smooth."

Speaker: Lysander

Significance: Establishes love as difficult even before magic intervenes.

"I am your spaniel."

Speaker: Helena

Significance: Shows Helena’s lack of self-respect and desperation.

"Now thou and I are new in amity."

Speaker: Oberon

Significance: Signals restoration of order and harmony.

How to ACE Speaker Identification

  • Dramatic & exaggerated = Mechanical
  • Legal language = Theseus or Egeus
  • Magic, nature, control = Oberon
  • Self-hate or devotion = Helena
  • Chaos or jokes = Puck
